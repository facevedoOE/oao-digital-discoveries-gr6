The Decomposition Tree Builder

In ENGAGE VR, you'll step into a 3D mind-map studio. You'll grab a giant problem out of thin air, slice it into floating sub-problems with your own hands, and rearrange them into a real decomposition tree you can walk around. About 20–25 minutes in headset.

VR Environment

ENGAGE Mind-Map Studio — a customizable open room with floating object trays, magnetic connector lines, and voice-to-text labels. Hosted on the Optima Academy classroom server. Compatible with Meta Quest 2/3, HTC Vive, and ENGAGE desktop mode if no headset is available.

📋 Before you put on the headset

  • Your weekend reflection from the reading (pick the big task you decomposed).
  • A blank sheet of paper or notes app — for quick capture between VR steps.
  • A clear 6-foot-by-6-foot space, with the headset and controllers charged.
  • The ENGAGE app launched and signed in to your Optima Academy classroom.

🎯 Session walkthrough

1
Enter the Mind-Map Studio
Pick the room labeled "Decomposition Tree — Lesson 7.2" from your classroom shelf. When you're inside, you'll see a glowing platform with a single empty card floating above it. The room is otherwise quiet and dim — that's by design.
2
Speak your big problem
Hold the trigger and say your big problem out loud — the same one you wrote down in your weekend reflection. The card above the platform will fill with your spoken words. (If your headset doesn't have a mic, type with the floating keyboard.)
3
Pull sub-problem cards out of the air
Reach toward the platform and pinch — a fresh blank card will materialize in your hand. Speak (or type) one sub-problem onto each card. Pull 4 to 6 cards total. Float them in a loose cloud above the original problem.
4
Connect with the magnetic line tool
Squeeze your trigger near the big problem card and drag toward each sub-problem card. A glowing blue line will snap from one to the other. Now you have a tree — the big problem on top, branches reaching down.
5
Walk around. Find the too-big branch.
Step back from the platform and walk a slow circle around your tree. Read each sub-problem out loud. Find the one branch that still feels too big to solve directly. Mark it by squeezing the grip button — it'll glow gold.
6
Decompose that branch again
Pinch beneath the gold-glowing card. New blank cards will appear. Decompose it into 2–4 smaller sub-problems. Connect them downward with the magnetic line tool. Now your tree has two levels — the second-level decomposition.
7
Snapshot & export
Open your wrist menu and tap the camera icon. ENGAGE will save a snapshot of your tree to the classroom drive. Take off the headset — the snapshot now lives in your Optima Academy folder, ready for the reflection below.

💭 After you take off the headset — Reflection

Answer all four questions below. Type your responses in the boxes — no minimum length, but be specific. When you're done, tap Submit Reflection at the bottom.

Q1. What was your big problem, and how many sub-problems did your final tree have (across both levels)?
Q2. Which branch was too big and needed a second-level decomposition? What was hiding inside it?
Q3. Walking around the tree in 3D — did you notice anything you wouldn't have noticed by just writing it on paper?
Q4. If a friend handed you this tree without context, would they understand it? What's one label you could make clearer?
