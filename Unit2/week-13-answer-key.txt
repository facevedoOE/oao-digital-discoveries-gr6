───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q1. What does the "A" for AUTHORITY in the CRAAP test ask
    you to check?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) When the article was last updated
     ❌ That's CURRENCY (the first C in CRAAP), not Authority.
  B) Whether the article fits your specific question
     ❌ That's RELEVANCE — the R in CRAAP.
  C) Who wrote it and what makes them an expert            ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Authority asks about the AUTHOR — their name,
        credentials, and qualification to write on the
        topic. (SC.6.HS.1.2)
  D) Why the website exists in the first place
     ❌ That's PURPOSE — the P in CRAAP.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q2. Which top-level domain (TLD) is restricted ONLY to U.S.
    government agencies?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) .com
     ❌ Anyone can register a .com — it's the most open TLD
        on the internet.
  B) .org
     ❌ Originally for non-profits, but now anyone can
        register .org.
  C) .gov                                                  ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Only U.S. government agencies can use .gov — like
        nasa.gov, usa.gov, weather.gov. (SC.6.HS.1.2)
  D) .edu
     ❌ .edu is restricted, but to U.S. accredited SCHOOLS
        (universities and some K-12), not to the government.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q3. What is LATERAL READING?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Reading the same article over and over until you
     remember it
     ❌ Re-reading the same page doesn't fact-check it —
        you need OTHER sources for that.
  B) Leaving the page to fact-check it on other trusted
     sources                                                ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Lateral reading means leaving the page and searching
        what other trusted sources say about the site or
        claim. Fact-checkers use this all the time.
        (SC.6.HS.1.2)
  C) Reading by holding the screen sideways
     ❌ Lateral reading isn't about screen orientation —
        it's about checking sources against each other.
  D) Skipping every other paragraph to save time
     ❌ That's skim-reading. Lateral reading means cross-
        checking with OTHER sources.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q4. A pop-up screams: "YOUR COMPUTER IS INFECTED — call
    1-800-FAKE-NOW immediately!" What is the BEST move?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Call the number to fix the problem
     ❌ Real Microsoft and Apple NEVER show pop-ups asking
        you to call a phone number. The pop-up itself is
        the scam.
  B) Pay them with a gift card so they help faster
     ❌ Gift-card payment to "tech support" is a flashing-
        red sign of a scam. Real companies don't take gift
        cards.
  C) Close the pop-up; don't call. If it won't close,
     force-quit the browser and tell a trusted adult.       ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Fake tech support pop-ups are common scams. Close,
        don't call, and ask a trusted adult if it won't
        close. (SC.6.HS.1.4)
  D) Click through every option in the pop-up to find a
     "decline" button
     ❌ Clicking through pop-ups often installs malware.
        Don't engage — just close.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q5. You're writing a 6th-grade report on the history of
    NASA. Which is the BEST source to cite?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) An anonymous fan blog with no dates and no citations
     ❌ No author, no dates, no citations — that fails most
        of CRAAP.
  B) A YouTube comment thread arguing about space
     ❌ Comments aren't sources — no author, no facts, no
        review.
  C) nasa.gov — NASA's own official .gov website           ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ NASA's own .gov website is a primary source — high
        authority on its own programs and citation-quality.
        (SC.6.HS.1.2)
  D) A Pinterest board with cool space pictures and no
     captions
     ❌ Pinterest re-shares images without source info.
        Not citation-quality.
