The Journey of Data

Every photo you save, every game score that loads, every Canvas message that arrives — it's all data on a journey. This week, you'll trace that journey from the moment data is created, through where it sleeps, all the way to the second it lands back on your screen.

◆ Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

1
Define data and identify the three states it can be in: at rest, in motion, and in use.
2
Compare local and cloud storage and explain when each one is the smarter choice.
3
Trace the journey a single piece of data takes from your device to a server and back.
4
Distinguish networks — LAN, WAN, and the Internet — and explain how data is retrieved from a database.
01
Section 1 · What Is Data, Really?

Every tap, swipe, and save is data

Open your phone or laptop and ask yourself: what just happened? A photo loaded. A song started. A friend's message popped up. Each of those moments is the result of data — facts, numbers, words, images, sounds, or any information a computer can store and use.

Data isn't just one thing. It exists in three different states, and each state matters for how we protect it, move it, and pay for it.

💾Data at Rest
Sitting on a hard drive, a phone, a USB stick, or a cloud server. Not moving, not being looked at — just stored. Example: a photo on your phone you haven't opened in months.
📡Data in Motion
Traveling between devices through Wi-Fi, cell signal, or cables. Example: a Canvas message moving from your laptop to your teacher's screen.
🧠Data in Use
Open in an app and being read, edited, or processed by a computer right now. Example: a Google Doc you're typing into this very second.
📘 Vocabulary

Data — facts, numbers, words, images, or sounds that a computer can store and use.

Data at rest — data being stored on a device or server (not moving, not open).

Data in motion — data traveling between devices over a network.

Data in use — data open in an app and being processed by a computer.

02
Section 2 · Where Data Sleeps — Storage

Local storage vs. cloud storage

When data is "at rest," it has to live somewhere — like a book sitting on a shelf. There are two big shelves where digital data sleeps: local storage (right on your device) and cloud storage (on a server far away that you reach through the internet).

📱 Local Storage
Lives on your device.
  • Hard drives (HDD), solid-state drives (SSD), USB sticks, SD cards
  • Works without internet — fast and private
  • If your device is lost or broken, the data is gone
  • Limited by the size of your device
☁️ Cloud Storage
Lives on a far-away server.
  • Google Drive, iCloud, OneDrive, Dropbox
  • Reach it from any device with internet
  • Your stuff survives even if your laptop dies
  • Needs a connection — and someone else holds your data
⚙️ Common Storage Devices
💽
HDD
Spinning disks. Cheap, big, slower.
⚡
SSD
No moving parts. Fast, quiet, pricier.
🔌
USB / SD
Tiny, portable, easy to lose.
☁️
Cloud
Remote servers. Reach it anywhere.

03
Section 3 · The Round Trip

From your screen to a server and back

When you upload a photo to Google Drive, the file doesn't just teleport. It takes a real journey across cables, antennas, and switches — and a tiny copy of it ends up "at rest" on a server you'll probably never see. Here's the round trip, step by step:

1
You hit Save (data is in use → in motion)
Your device packages up the photo and gets it ready to leave — file size, file name, your account info, all attached.
2
It hops through your home network to the internet
Wi-Fi → router → modem → your internet provider's network. From there, it's on the public internet.
3
It arrives at a server in a data center
A server is a powerful computer built to serve data to thousands of people at once. It writes the photo to a drive — now it's at rest.
4
Tomorrow, you ask for it back
Open the app on a different device. The server hears your request, finds the file, and sends a copy back across the same kind of journey.
5
The photo loads on your screen (back in use)
Three states. One round trip. All in just a few seconds.
💭 Think About It

Classical thinkers prized the virtue of prudence — knowing the right action for the right moment. Choosing where data sleeps is a small act of prudence: the wrong choice can cost you privacy, speed, or even the data itself. A wise digital citizen learns to ask, "Where should this live, and why?"

04
Section 4 · Networks Big and Small

LAN, WAN, and the Internet

Data in motion travels along a network — a group of devices linked together so they can share information. Networks come in different sizes, and each size has a name:

🏠LAN — Local Area Network
Devices in one place — your house, your school, a single coffee shop. Connected by Wi-Fi or short cables. Example: your laptop, phone, and smart TV all using the same home Wi-Fi.
🌆WAN — Wide Area Network
Connects LANs across cities, states, or countries. Example: a school district with five campuses sharing one student records system.
🌐The Internet
The biggest WAN of all — billions of devices and millions of networks, linked across the entire planet. Example: visiting a website hosted in another country.
💡 Did You Know?

Most of the internet doesn't actually live in the sky. Around 95% of international internet traffic travels through thick fiber-optic cables lying on the ocean floor. There are over 500 of these undersea cables crisscrossing the planet right now — your TikTok video may have crossed an ocean before reaching you.

05
Section 5 · Finding the Right File

Storing data is half the job — finding it is the other half

Imagine a school library with one billion books and no organization system. That's basically what the internet is — except it has to give you the right book in less than a second. The trick? Databases and queries.

DB
A database is data stored in a smart, searchable structure
Think of a giant spreadsheet — rows and columns — but built so a computer can scan millions of entries in milliseconds.
?
A query is a question you ask the database
When you search Canvas for "ancient Rome," your search words become a query — a set of instructions that tells the database what to find.
↩
Results travel back as data in motion
The database hands the matches back to the app, which sends them to your screen. The data leaves "rest," moves, and ends up "in use" — all in one click.
🌎 Real-World Connection

Hospitals run on databases. When a doctor pulls up your medical records, they're sending a query to a database that may hold tens of millions of patient files. The right data has to arrive in seconds — and only the right people are allowed to see it.

The same kind of data journey you'll trace this week is what keeps doctors, pilots, banks, and search engines working every minute of every day.
