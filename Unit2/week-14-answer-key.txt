───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q1. Which of the following is the BEST example of ACTIVE
    screen time?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Endlessly scrolling Reels and Shorts
     ❌ Endless scrolling is the textbook example of
        PASSIVE screen time, not active.
  B) Watching reaction videos auto-play in the background
     ❌ Background auto-play is the most passive form of
        screen use.
  C) Coding a small project or video-calling Grandma       ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Coding (creating) and video-calling (genuinely
        connecting) are textbook active screen time.
        (SC.6.HS.2.2)
  D) Falling asleep with a movie on
     ❌ That's passive — and combined with sleep
        disruption, it's a wellness double-whammy.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q2. Why does using your phone in bed at night make it
    harder to fall asleep?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) The phone is too heavy to hold for long
     ❌ Phone weight isn't the issue. The issue is light
        affecting your sleep hormones.
  B) Blue light from the screen suppresses melatonin and
     tells your brain it's daytime                          ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Blue light suppresses melatonin and pushes back
        the sleep signal in your brain. (SC.6.HS.2.2)
  C) Phones drain the oxygen in your room
     ❌ Phones don't change room oxygen. The mechanism is
        light, not air.
  D) Apps secretly broadcast caffeine
     ❌ Not a real thing. The real mechanism is blue-light
        suppression of melatonin.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q3. What does the 20-20-20 rule recommend for digital eye
    strain?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Use 20 different apps every 20 minutes
     ❌ App-switching has nothing to do with eye strain.
        The rule is about distance breaks.
  B) Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for
     20 seconds                                             ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Quick distance breaks every 20 minutes give your
        eye muscles a chance to relax. (SC.6.HS.2.2)
  C) Limit yourself to 20 minutes of screen time per day
     ❌ The rule isn't a daily total — it's a break
        schedule for whatever screen time you DO have.
  D) Look at a screen for 20 hours and then take 20 minutes
     off
     ❌ That's way too much screen time. The rule is much
        smaller and more frequent.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q4. What is DOOMSCROLLING?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) A new video game where you scroll through dungeons
     ❌ Doomscrolling isn't a video game — it's a real-
        world habit researchers study.
  B) Scrolling through bad-news headlines, often late at
     night, until you feel anxious                          ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Doomscrolling is reading bad news after bad news,
        often late at night, leaving you anxious about
        things that aren't actually happening to you.
        (SC.6.HS.2.2)
  C) Scrolling really, really fast on a touchscreen
     ❌ Scroll speed has nothing to do with the term. It's
        about WHAT you scroll, not HOW fast.
  D) A built-in phone setting that locks your screen
     ❌ Doomscrolling is a behavior, not a phone setting.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q5. Which is the BEST habit for keeping your phone from
    wrecking your sleep?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) "I'll try to use my phone less at night"
     ❌ That's a wish, not a habit. Vague intentions
        rarely change behavior.
  B) Plug your phone in across the room at 9 p.m. and use
     a real alarm clock                                     ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Concrete, specific, and removes the temptation.
        That's how habits actually stick. (SC.6.HS.2.2)
  C) Sleep with your phone under your pillow but try not
     to check it
     ❌ If the phone is right there, you'll check it.
        Distance is the whole point.
  D) Put your phone face-down right next to your bed
     ❌ Better than face-up, but the phone is still right
        there to grab. Across-the-room is stronger.
