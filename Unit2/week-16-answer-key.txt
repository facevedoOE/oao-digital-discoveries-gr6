───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q1. You're typing into a Google Doc, words appearing as you
    type. What state is the document data in?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Data at rest
     ❌ At rest means stored and not active. The doc is
        open and changing right now.
  B) Data in motion
     ❌ In motion means traveling on a network. Editing is
        processing, not traveling.
  C) Data in use                                            ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Data being read or processed by an open app is in
        use. (SC.6.CS.1.1)
  D) Data in transit
     ❌ "In transit" is another way to say in motion.
        Editing isn't traveling.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q2. What is the PRINCIPLE OF LEAST PRIVILEGE?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Always give every user the highest possible role so
     nothing breaks
     ❌ That's the OPPOSITE of least privilege — and a
        recipe for accidents and leaks.
  B) Give every user exactly the access they need to do
     their job — and nothing more                           ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Match the role to the real need — no extra access.
        (SC.6.CS.1.2)
  C) Only the IT admin should ever have access to anything
     ❌ That would block real work. Least privilege means
        right-sizing, not eliminating.
  D) Default to "Anyone with the link" so people don't
     have to ask
     ❌ That's the most-privilege setting, not least.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q3. Which is the STRONGEST password?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) password123
     ❌ One of the most common passwords. Cracked in under
        a second.
  B) orange-lamp-tiger-cloud-9                              ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ A long passphrase with random words and a number is
        hard to guess and brute-force. (SC.6.CS.2.1)
  C) qwerty
     ❌ Top 5 letters on a keyboard. Computers try this
        first.
  D) your first name + the year you were born
     ❌ Way too easy to guess if a phisher knows even a
        little about you.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q4. What does the "s" in https:// tell you about a website
    connection?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) It means the site is faster than other sites
     ❌ The "s" has nothing to do with speed. It stands
        for SECURE.
  B) It means the connection is encrypted (secure) so
     data is scrambled while it travels                     ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ HTTPS means encryption is protecting the
        connection. (SC.6.CS.1.5)
  C) Nothing — http and https are exactly the same
     ❌ HTTPS is encrypted; HTTP is plaintext. Big
        difference.
  D) It means the site is owned by the school
     ❌ The "s" doesn't say anything about who owns the
        site.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q5. What is LATERAL READING?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Reading the same article over and over until you
     remember it
     ❌ Re-reading the same page doesn't fact-check it.
  B) Leaving the page to fact-check it on other trusted
     sources                                                ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Lateral reading means leaving the page and
        searching what other trusted sources say.
        (SC.6.HS.1.2)
  C) Reading by holding the screen sideways
     ❌ Lateral reading isn't about screen orientation.
  D) Skipping every other paragraph to save time
     ❌ That's skim-reading. Lateral reading means cross-
        checking with OTHER sources.
