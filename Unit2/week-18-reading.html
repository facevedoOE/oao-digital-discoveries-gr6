Section 1 · Why Reflection Matters

Looking back is how you grow forward

Athletes watch game film. Pilots run debriefs. Surgeons review every operation. They don't do it because they failed — they do it because reflection is how skilled people get better. Without it, you keep making the same mistakes and missing the same opportunities.

In school, this kind of thinking has a name: metacognition — "thinking about your own thinking." When you ask "what did I learn? what was hard? what would I do differently?" — you're not just remembering. You're building a more capable future you.

📘 Reflection Vocabulary

Reflection — pausing to think carefully about your learning and your choices.

Metacognition — thinking about your own thinking — what you know, how you learned it, and what's still unclear.

Portfolio — a curated collection of your best work that shows what you can do.

Growth mindset — the belief that your skills get better with effort, practice, and good feedback.

Section 2 · Unit 1 Recap (Weeks 1–8)

Communication, Multimedia & Files

Your first eight weeks built the foundation: how to communicate well online, how to make multimedia that lands, and how to keep your digital work organized.

Lesson 1 · Communication
Synchronous vs asynchronous, channels, tone, packets and routers. The 3 C's: clarity, concision, courtesy.
Lesson 2 · Multimedia
Purpose & audience first, then design. Inform / persuade / entertain — and pick the format that serves the message.
Lesson 3 · Files & Folders
File formats, naming conventions, folder structure, cloud vs local trade-offs. Future-you will thank present-you.
Project & Assessment
Multimedia mini-project + Unit 1 assessment. Your first time bringing skills together for a real audience.
U2
Section 3 · Unit 2 Recap (Weeks 9–17)

Data & Cyber Safety

Your second unit went deeper: where data lives, who can touch it, and how to protect yourself in a world full of phishers, scammers, and infinite scrolls.

Lesson 4 · Data & Permissions
Three states of data. Local vs cloud. LAN/WAN/Internet. Roles, share scopes, and the principle of least privilege.
Lesson 5 · Safety & Identity
Phishing tells, social-engineering levers, public/private/confidential, strong passwords, MFA, encryption, HTTPS, footprint.
Lesson 6 · Sources & Wellness
CRAAP test, TLDs, lateral reading, scam-spotting, active vs passive screen time, the 20-20-20 rule, balance habits.
Project & Assessment
Cyber Safety PSA — you taught what you learned. Plus the 15-question Unit 2 Assessment.
💭 Think About It

Three classical virtues ran through both units:

  • Prudence — knowing when to send, what to share, who to trust.
  • Justice — giving each person the right access, no more, no less.
  • Temperance — self-control with screens, time, and your own attention.

Which one showed up most for you this semester?
