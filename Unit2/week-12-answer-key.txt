Q1. What is the MAIN PURPOSE of encryption?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) To make data load faster on the internet
     ❌ Encryption isn't about speed — it adds a small bit
        of work, not less.
  B) To scramble a message so only the right person can
     read it                                                ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Encryption turns plaintext into ciphertext that's
        unreadable without the key. (SC.6.CS.1.5)
  C) To delete old messages automatically
     ❌ Encryption doesn't delete anything — it scrambles
        messages so only the right person can read them.
  D) To store data permanently in the cloud
     ❌ That's cloud storage. Encryption is about protecting
        messages, not where they live.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q2. Using a Caesar cipher with a shift of 3, what does the
    ciphertext FDW decrypt to (shift each letter back 3)?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) DOG
     ❌ Shift each letter back 3: F→C, D→A, W→T = CAT,
        not DOG.
  B) CAT                                                   ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ F→C, D→A, W→T. The plaintext is CAT.
        (SC.6.CS.1.5)
  C) BAT
     ❌ Close, but B is shift-back 4 from F. With shift 3,
        F goes back to C — so the answer starts with C, not B.
  D) CAR
     ❌ First two letters are right (CA), but W shifts back
        3 to T — not R.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q3. What does the "s" in https:// tell you about a website
    connection?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) It means the site is faster than other sites
     ❌ The "s" has nothing to do with speed. It stands for
        SECURE.
  B) It means the connection is encrypted (secure) so data
     is scrambled while it travels                          ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ HTTPS means encryption is protecting the connection.
        Without HTTPS, your password would travel as
        plaintext. (SC.6.CS.1.5)
  C) It means the site is owned by the school
     ❌ The "s" doesn't say anything about who owns the
        site — it's about encryption.
  D) Nothing — http and https are exactly the same
     ❌ HTTPS and HTTP are very different. HTTPS is
        encrypted; HTTP is plaintext.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q4. Which of these is the SAFEST username for a public game
    leaderboard?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) SophiaMartinez_2014
     ❌ Real first name + last name + birth year is way too
        much PII for a public profile.
  B) SophiaM_OakRidgeMS
     ❌ Real name + the school you attend is enough for a
        stranger to find you in real life.
  C) BlueDragon42                                          ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Memorable, but no real name, school, or birthday —
        keeps your identity private. (SC.6.CS.1.6)
  D) 7thGrade_Rm104_Sophia
     ❌ Even more dangerous — that points to a specific
        school, classroom, AND name.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q5. Which of these is an example of a PASSIVE digital
    footprint — data collected automatically, not posted by
    you on purpose?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) A discussion-board reply you typed
     ❌ That's an ACTIVE footprint — you wrote and posted
        it on purpose.
  B) A photo you uploaded to your profile
     ❌ Active — you chose to upload it.
  C) A list of YouTube videos you've watched, tracked by
     the app                                                ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ You didn't post your watch history — the app
        collected it automatically. That's a passive
        footprint. (SC.6.CS.1.6)
  D) A bio sentence you wrote on your profile
     ❌ Active — those words went up because you typed
        them.
