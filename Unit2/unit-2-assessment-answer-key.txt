───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q1. Which of the following is the BEST example of DATA IN
    MOTION?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) A photo file sitting in your phone's camera roll,
     untouched for months
     ❌ That's data AT REST — stored on a device and not
        moving.
  B) A Canvas message traveling from your laptop, through
     Wi-Fi, to your teacher's screen                        ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Data traveling between devices over a network is
        in motion. (SC.6.CS.1.1)
  C) A Google Doc that's open and you're typing into it
     ❌ That's data IN USE — open in an app and being
        processed.
  D) An mp3 song saved to a USB stick on your desk
     ❌ That's data AT REST — stored on a USB drive, not
        moving.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q2. You're heading to a remote campsite for a week with no
    Wi-Fi. You want to listen to 50 of your favorite songs.
    Which storage choice makes the most sense?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Stream the songs from a cloud music service
     ❌ Streaming needs an internet connection — which you
        won't have at the campsite.
  B) Save the songs in cloud storage like Google Drive
     ❌ Cloud storage also needs internet to reach the
        files. Same problem as streaming.
  C) Download the songs to local storage on your phone     ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Local storage works without internet. Once they're
        on your device, you can play them anywhere.
        (SC.6.CS.1.1)
  D) Email the songs to yourself before you leave
     ❌ Even if the email arrived, you'd still need
        internet to open the attachments.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q3. A school district has five separate campuses, all
    sharing one student-records system. Which type of
    network connects the campuses?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) LAN (Local Area Network)
     ❌ A LAN connects devices in ONE place — like a
        single building. Five campuses are too far apart.
  B) WAN (Wide Area Network)                              ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ A WAN connects multiple LANs across cities,
        states, or countries — like a five-campus
        district. (SC.6.CS.1.1)
  C) A USB connection
     ❌ USB connects two devices physically. It can't
        reach across multiple campuses.
  D) A database
     ❌ A database is an organized collection of data —
        not a type of network.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q4. You finish a personal essay and want your dad to READ
    it but NOT change any of your sentences. Which role
    should you give him?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Owner
     ❌ Owner has full control — including delete and
        re-share. Way more than needed for "just read it."
  B) Editor
     ❌ Editor can change your sentences. You said you
        don't want him doing that.
  C) Viewer                                               ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Viewer is read-only — he can open and read but
        cannot change anything. (SC.6.CS.1.2)
  D) Commenter
     ❌ Commenter could leave comments on your file. If
        you only want him to read silently, Viewer is the
        tighter choice.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q5. Which sharing setting is the MOST RISKY for a private
    file you don't want strangers to see?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Specific people, role: Viewer
     ❌ Very safe — only the people you list can find it,
        and they can only read.
  B) Anyone in your school, role: Viewer
     ❌ Some risk, since everyone in your school can find
        it — but the public still can't.
  C) Anyone with the link, role: Editor                   ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ The most dangerous combo — anyone who gets the
        link can not only read but also change your file.
        (SC.6.CS.1.2)
  D) Specific people, role: Editor
     ❌ Reasonable for a small trusted group — only the
        named people can find it, even if they can edit.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q6. A DM says: "Hi, I work at Discord. Send me your
    password AND your 6-digit MFA code right now or
    you'll lose your account!" What's the right move?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Send the password and code so you don't lose the
     account
     ❌ Real Discord employees NEVER ask for your
        password or MFA code. The whole point of those
        is that you keep them secret.
  B) Don't send anything. Block, report, and tell a
     trusted adult.                                       ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Authority + urgency + asking for password and
        MFA code = textbook phishing. Block, report, tell
        a trusted adult. (SC.6.CS.1.3)
  C) Send only the password — that's safer
     ❌ Still a no. Even one piece of confidential info
        is too much to give a stranger.
  D) Reply asking for proof they're really from Discord
     ❌ Don't engage with phishers — anything you say
        can be used to keep the trick going. Just block
        and report.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q7. Which of the following should be classified as
    CONFIDENTIAL information — never shared with anyone
    online, no matter who claims to be asking?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Your favorite music genre
     ❌ Music tastes are PUBLIC — totally fine to share.
  B) The name of your school
     ❌ A school name is generally PUBLIC — printed on
        jerseys, posted on websites.
  C) Your password and the 6-digit MFA code that just
     texted to your phone                                  ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Passwords and MFA codes are CONFIDENTIAL. Real
        companies will NEVER ask for them. (SC.6.CS.1.4)
  D) Your favorite NBA team
     ❌ Sports preferences are PUBLIC — already visible
        on profiles or already known.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q8. What does multi-factor authentication (MFA) protect
    you against, even if a scammer steals your password?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Slow internet speeds
     ❌ MFA isn't about speed — it's about identity.
  B) Account takeover by an attacker who has only your
     password                                              ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Even if a phisher steals your password, they
        can't log in without the second factor (a code
        on your phone). (SC.6.CS.2.1)
  C) Lost device damage and broken screens
     ❌ MFA doesn't protect physical devices — it
        protects access to your accounts.
  D) Battery drain on your phone
     ❌ MFA has nothing to do with battery life.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q9. What is the MAIN PURPOSE of encryption?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) To make data load faster on the internet
     ❌ Encryption isn't about speed — it adds a small
        bit of work, not less.
  B) To scramble a message so only the right person can
     read it                                               ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Encryption turns plaintext into ciphertext that's
        unreadable without the key. (SC.6.CS.1.5)
  C) To delete old messages automatically
     ❌ Encryption doesn't delete anything — it
        scrambles messages so only the right person can
        read them.
  D) To store data permanently in the cloud
     ❌ That's cloud storage. Encryption is about
        protecting messages, not where they live.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q10. Using a Caesar cipher with a shift of 3, what does
     the ciphertext FDW decrypt to (shift each letter
     back 3)?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) DOG
     ❌ Shift each letter back 3: F→C, D→A, W→T = CAT,
        not DOG.
  B) CAT                                                   ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ F→C, D→A, W→T. The plaintext is CAT.
        (SC.6.CS.1.5)
  C) BAT
     ❌ Close, but B is shift-back 4 from F. With shift
        3, F goes back to C.
  D) CAR
     ❌ First two letters are right (CA), but W shifts
        back 3 to T — not R.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q11. Which of these is an example of a PASSIVE digital
     footprint — data collected automatically, not
     posted by you on purpose?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) A discussion-board reply you typed
     ❌ That's an ACTIVE footprint — you wrote and
        posted it on purpose.
  B) A photo you uploaded to your profile
     ❌ Active — you chose to upload it.
  C) A list of YouTube videos you've watched, tracked
     by the app                                            ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ You didn't post your watch history — the app
        collected it automatically. That's a passive
        footprint. (SC.6.CS.1.6)
  D) A bio sentence you wrote on your profile
     ❌ Active — those words went up because you typed
        them.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q12. Which top-level domain (TLD) is restricted ONLY to
     U.S. government agencies?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) .com
     ❌ Anyone can register a .com — it's the most open
        TLD.
  B) .org
     ❌ Originally for non-profits, but now anyone can
        register .org.
  C) .gov                                                  ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Only U.S. government agencies can use .gov —
        like nasa.gov, usa.gov, weather.gov.
        (SC.6.HS.1.2)
  D) .edu
     ❌ .edu is restricted to U.S. accredited SCHOOLS,
        not the government.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q13. A pop-up screams: "YOUR COMPUTER IS INFECTED — call
     1-800-FAKE-NOW immediately!" What is the BEST move?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Call the number to fix the problem
     ❌ Real Microsoft and Apple NEVER show pop-ups
        asking you to call a phone number.
  B) Close the pop-up; don't call. If it won't close,
     force-quit the browser and tell a trusted adult.      ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Fake tech-support pop-ups are common scams.
        (SC.6.HS.1.4)
  C) Pay them with a gift card so they help faster
     ❌ Gift-card payment to "tech support" is a
        flashing-red sign of a scam.
  D) Click through every option in the pop-up
     ❌ Clicking through pop-ups often installs malware.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q14. Which of the following is the BEST example of
     ACTIVE screen time?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Endlessly scrolling Reels and Shorts
     ❌ Endless scrolling is the textbook example of
        PASSIVE screen time.
  B) Watching reaction videos auto-play in the
     background
     ❌ Background auto-play is the most passive form
        of screen use.
  C) Coding a small project or video-calling Grandma     ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Coding (creating) and video-calling (genuinely
        connecting) are textbook active screen time.
        (SC.6.HS.2.2)
  D) Falling asleep with a movie on
     ❌ That's passive — and combined with sleep
        disruption, a wellness double-whammy.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q15. Why does using your phone in bed at night make it
     harder to fall asleep?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) The phone is too heavy to hold for long
     ❌ Phone weight isn't the issue.
  B) Blue light from the screen suppresses melatonin
     and tells your brain it's daytime                     ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Blue light suppresses melatonin and pushes back
        the sleep signal in your brain. (SC.6.HS.2.2)
  C) Phones drain the oxygen in your room
     ❌ Phones don't change room oxygen.
  D) Apps secretly broadcast caffeine
     ❌ Not a real thing.
