UNIT 2 • WEEK 15 • PROJECT RUBRIC

Cyber Safety PSA — Rubric & Checklist

Use this page two ways: (1) read the rubric BEFORE you build, so you know exactly what "exemplary" looks like; (2) run the checklist BEFORE you submit, so you don't lose points on something fixable.

Project Total
40 points
4 criteria × 10 points each. Weight is even — you can't make up a weak fact base with great visuals (or vice versa).
01
Section 1 · The Rubric — 4 Criteria × 4 Levels

What "exemplary" actually looks like

1
Content Accuracy
Are your facts right? Tied to a real Unit 2 standard?
/ 10
Exemplary · 9–10
All 3 facts are accurate, well-explained, and clearly tied to a Unit 2 lesson or standard. No confusion about the topic.
Proficient · 7–8
Facts are accurate but one or two are vague or only half-explained. Topic is still clear.
Developing · 5–6
One fact is incorrect or misleading. Topic is mostly clear but the message wobbles.
Beginning · 0–4
Facts are missing or wrong. Topic is unclear or off-standard. PSA does not teach what it claims to teach.
2
Craft & Clarity
Do visuals, language, and layout serve the message?
/ 10
Exemplary · 9–10
All 4 PSA parts (hook/facts/story/CTA) are clearly present. Visuals and words work together. Polished, focused, easy to follow.
Proficient · 7–8
All 4 parts present but one is weaker (e.g., a vague hook). Layout is mostly clean. Minor typos.
Developing · 5–6
One part is missing or hard to find. Visuals are present but cluttered or off-topic. Multiple typos.
Beginning · 0–4
Multiple PSA parts missing. Layout is hard to read. Message gets lost.
3
Audience Fit
Could a 4th-grader understand AND act on this?
/ 10
Exemplary · 9–10
Language is age-appropriate. CTA is one specific habit a 4th-grader could start tomorrow. Examples connect to their world.
Proficient · 7–8
Language mostly fits the audience. CTA is clear but slightly generic. Examples are okay.
Developing · 5–6
Some language is too advanced or jargon-heavy. CTA is vague ("be safe online"). Examples don't connect.
Beginning · 0–4
Language and examples don't fit a younger student. No clear call-to-action.
4
Citation & Sourcing
Are facts cited with CRAAP-quality sources?
/ 10
Exemplary · 9–10
All 3 facts have a clear, named source (lesson, .gov, expert). Sources pass the CRAAP test from Week 13.
Proficient · 7–8
All 3 facts cited but one source is borderline (e.g., a .com you didn't lateral-read). Sourcing is mostly responsible.
Developing · 5–6
Only some facts cited. Sources are weak (anonymous blog, no date) or vague ("the internet").
Beginning · 0–4
No citations at all. Facts could be made up — there's no way for a viewer to check.

Section 3 · Required: Designer's Note

A 3-sentence note that frames your choices

📝 Required: Designer's Note

Submit a 3-sentence note with your PSA that answers:

  1. WHO is your audience?
  2. WHAT is the ONE behavior change you want from them?
  3. WHY did you pick this format over the others?

A clear designer's note makes grading fair and shows you thought about your choices.
