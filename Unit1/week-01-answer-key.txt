───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q1. Which of the following is the BEST example of SYNCHRONOUS
    digital communication?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Posting a reply to a discussion board the night before class
     ❌ Discussion posts are asynchronous — they're read whenever
        the reader gets to them, not live.
  B) Sending an email to your teacher about homework
     ❌ Email is asynchronous — sender and receiver are not online
        at the same time.
  C) Joining a live video class with your teacher and classmates  ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Live video calls are synchronous: everyone is present and
        communicating in real time. (SC.6.CC.1.1)
  D) Leaving a voicemail for a friend
     ❌ A voicemail is asynchronous — the friend listens later,
        not in real time.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q2. What is a DATA PACKET?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) A folder that stores all of your digital files
     ❌ That describes storage or a directory — not a packet.
  B) A password that protects your messages
     ❌ That's encryption or authentication, not a packet.
  C) A small chunk of information that travels across a network  ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Data packets are small pieces of a larger message. They
        travel separately and are reassembled at the destination.
        (SC.6.CC.1.1)
  D) A type of router used to connect devices
     ❌ Routers DIRECT packets — they aren't packets themselves.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q3. You need to send your science teacher a detailed question
    about a project. Which tool is MOST appropriate?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) A quick text with abbreviations
     ❌ Too informal for a teacher, and texting class questions is
        rarely the right channel.
  B) An email written in a clear, polite tone                    ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Email is asynchronous, professional, and leaves a written
        record — ideal for a detailed question to a teacher.
        (SC.6.CC.1.1)
  C) A reaction emoji on a class post
     ❌ An emoji can't carry a full question. The teacher won't
        know what you're asking.
  D) A live video call at midnight
     ❌ Synchronous calls require both people present — and late
        at night is not appropriate for a teacher.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q4. Which statement BEST describes ASYNCHRONOUS communication?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Both people must be online at the same time
     ❌ That describes SYNCHRONOUS, not asynchronous.
  B) Messages are sent and received at different times, not live  ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ The prefix "a-" means "not" — asynchronous = not together
        in time. Emails and discussion posts are classic examples.
        (SC.6.CC.1.1)
  C) Asynchronous communication only works for video calls
     ❌ Video calls are typically synchronous — opposite of
        asynchronous.
  D) Asynchronous communication needs a special network
     ❌ Asynchronous tools use the same networks as synchronous
        tools; the difference is timing, not hardware.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q5. In digital communication, what is the role of a ROUTER?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) It creates new messages for the sender
     ❌ Routers don't write messages — they move them.
  B) It permanently stores all data packets on a server
     ❌ That's storage/archiving, not routing.
  C) It directs data packets to the correct destination           ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ A router is like a digital traffic cop — it reads each
        packet's destination and sends it along the fastest
        available route. (SC.6.CC.1.1)
  D) It converts text messages into voice messages
     ❌ That's voice-to-text software, not a router.
