Q1. AESTHETIC UNITY in a multimedia project means:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Every page uses different colors and fonts to keep things
     interesting
     ❌ The opposite! Variety on every page kills unity. The
        whole point is consistent choices across pages.
  B) Including as many colors and fonts as possible
     ❌ More isn't better. Aesthetic unity comes from RESTRAINT
        — a small fixed palette and font pair.
  C) Every page of the design feels like part of the same       ✓ CORRECT
     project — same colors, same fonts, same layout style
     ✅ Right! Aesthetic unity is the visual signal that one
        designer made one project. (SC.6.CC.2.1)
  D) Using the most expensive design software available
     ❌ Tools don't create unity. Discipline does. You can make
        unified work in any tool, including free ones.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q2. The recommended size of a project COLOR PALETTE is:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) At least 10 colors, the more the better
     ❌ Too many colors creates visual chaos. Real designers
        keep palettes small.
  B) Exactly 1 color
     ❌ One color is too restrictive — you can't show emphasis,
        backgrounds, or accents.
  C) About 3–5 colors used consistently across the project     ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Right! 3–5 colors is the sweet spot — enough variety to
        show emphasis, few enough to feel unified. (SC.6.CC.2.1)
  D) A new color for every page
     ❌ A new palette per page is the opposite of aesthetic
        unity.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q3. Which of these is the STRONGEST piece of peer feedback?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) "Good job!"
     ❌ Vague — your classmate doesn't know what to keep doing
        or how to improve.
  B) "On page 2, the text is hard to read against the photo —  ✓ CORRECT
     could you add a darker background behind the words?"
     ✅ Specific (names the page and the problem), kind (offers
        a solution), and actionable. Strong Glow/Grow.
        (SC.6.CC.2.1)
  C) "It's bad."
     ❌ Vague AND unkind. Tells your classmate nothing useful.
  D) "Change everything."
     ❌ Overwhelming and not specific. What needs to change?
        How? Strong feedback names ONE thing.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q4. You and a teammate are co-editing a Canva project. You
    disagree with their photo choice. The BEST first action is:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Silently delete and replace it
     ❌ Silently overwriting a teammate's work breaks trust.
        Always communicate first.
  B) Stop working on the project
     ❌ Quitting on a small disagreement is unfair to your
        teammate and the work.
  C) Leave a Canva comment explaining your concern and         ✓ CORRECT
     suggesting an alternative
     ✅ Comments protect collaboration. They give your teammate
        a chance to respond before changes happen. (SC.6.CC.2.1)
  D) Tell the teacher first
     ❌ Save the teacher for big issues. Small disagreements
        are best handled inside the team using comments.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q5. The 6th item on the FINAL QUALITY CHECKLIST asks you to
    confirm:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Page count is at least 10
     ❌ More pages doesn't mean better. Page count isn't on
        the checklist.
  B) Background music is exactly 30 seconds
     ❌ Music length isn't a checklist item. Integration covers
        whether it FITS the project.
  C) Every collaborator is credited and any borrowed images    ✓ CORRECT
     or sounds are attributed
     ✅ Right! Honest credit (justice in action) and attribution
        of borrowed work are non-negotiable for honest design.
        (SC.6.CC.2.1)
  D) The project took at least one week to make
     ❌ Time isn't a checklist item. A great project is great
        whether it took 30 minutes or 3 weeks.
