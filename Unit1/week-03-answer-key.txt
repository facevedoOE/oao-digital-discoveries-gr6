Q1. What does "multimedia" mean?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Content that uses only video
     ❌ Video alone is one medium. Multimedia means MORE than
        one type combined.
  B) Content that uses only audio
     ❌ Audio alone is one medium. Multimedia combines two or
        more types on purpose.
  C) Content that combines two or more types of media (text,    ✓ CORRECT
     images, audio, video) on purpose
     ✅ Right! "Multi-" means "many." Multimedia is many media
        types working together. (SC.6.CC.2.1)
  D) Content that uses only text
     ❌ Text alone is one medium. The whole point of multimedia
        is combining types.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q2. The three classical RHETORICAL PURPOSES of communication —
    first identified by Aristotle — are:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Inform, confuse, frustrate
     ❌ "Confuse" and "frustrate" are not real rhetorical
        purposes — and good designers avoid both!
  B) Inform, persuade, entertain                                ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Aristotle's framework, still used today: teach the
        audience (inform), change what they think or do
        (persuade), or delight them (entertain). (SC.6.CC.2.1)
  C) Type, click, scroll
     ❌ Those are user actions, not communication purposes.
  D) Sketch, code, present
     ❌ Those are creative steps, not rhetorical purposes.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q3. You're designing a slideshow for KINDERGARTEN students.
    Which choice shows the BEST audience awareness?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Long paragraphs of text on every slide
     ❌ Kindergarteners can't read paragraphs. Wrong audience.
  B) A 20-page report with footnotes
     ❌ Reports with footnotes fit a college audience, not
        5-year-olds.
  C) Big, friendly images and one short sentence per slide      ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Audience-first design: big visuals + simple language
        match a kindergarten audience perfectly. (SC.6.CC.2.1)
  D) A black-and-white scientific diagram with no labels
     ❌ No engagement and no help reading — totally mismatched
        to a kindergarten audience.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q4. The MAIN reason designers create a STORYBOARD before they
    open any digital software is:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Storyboards are required by school rules
     ❌ Storyboards aren't required by rules — they're chosen
        by smart designers because they save time and effort.
  B) Sketches are cheap to change; finished video work is       ✓ CORRECT
     expensive to redo
     ✅ Right! That's why every studio in the world uses
        storyboards: catch problems while changes still cost
        seconds, not hours. (SC.6.CC.2.1)
  C) Storyboards look prettier than the final project
     ❌ Storyboards are usually rough on purpose. The point is
        planning, not beauty.
  D) Storyboards are faster to make than the final project
     ❌ True! But the deeper reason is changes are cheap at
        this stage — that's why we storyboard FIRST.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q5. INTEGRATION in multimedia design means:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Adding more ingredients than necessary
     ❌ More isn't better — integration is about how the parts
        work together, not how many you have.
  B) Using exactly the same color throughout
     ❌ That's one tiny aspect of style. Integration is much
        bigger — it's about all ingredients supporting one idea.
  C) When all the ingredients of a project (text, visuals,      ✓ CORRECT
     audio) support the SAME idea instead of fighting each
     other
     ✅ Exactly! Integrated work feels coherent — every
        ingredient says the same thing. (SC.6.CC.2.1)
  D) Removing all images so only text remains
     ❌ That's not integration — that's deletion. Integration
        keeps the ingredients but makes them work together.
