Q1. What does "netiquette" mean?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) A special kind of internet connection
     ❌ That would describe hardware or network speed — not a
        behavior rule.
  B) The unwritten rules of respectful online communication       ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Right! "Netiquette" blends "network" and "etiquette" —
        it's the code of polite online conduct. (SC.6.CC.1.1)
  C) A software that blocks bad messages
     ❌ That describes a filter or moderation tool, not
        netiquette.
  D) The name of a popular social media app
     ❌ It's not an app — it's a set of behavior norms.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q2. The 3 C's of effective digital writing are:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Creativity, caution, curiosity
     ❌ Those are great qualities, but not the 3 C's.
  B) Color, contrast, clarity
     ❌ Clarity is correct — but color and contrast belong to
        visual design, not writing.
  C) Clarity, concision, courtesy                                 ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Clarity (be understood), concision (respect the reader's
        time), courtesy (kind tone). (SC.6.CC.1.1)
  D) Confidence, charm, cleverness
     ❌ Nice qualities for a conversation, but these are not the
        3 C's of digital writing.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q3. You just wrote an angry reply to a discussion post. What is
    the WISEST action?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Save it as a draft, wait at least 30 minutes, and reread    ✓ CORRECT
     before sending
     ✅ This is the classic practice of TEMPERANCE — pausing
        before reacting. If the message still fits after the
        break, send. If not, you saved yourself. (SC.6.CC.1.1)
  B) Post it right away while you still feel strongly
     ❌ Messages written in strong emotion almost always sound
        worse the next day. This breaks temperance.
  C) Send it privately to a few friends first
     ❌ Now multiple people have a copy they could share. Not a
        safer path — it's a riskier one.
  D) Type it in ALL CAPS so your point is clear
     ❌ ALL CAPS reads like shouting and breaks netiquette —
        this makes the problem worse, not better.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q4. Which version of "I can't make practice today" has the
    BEST tone for a TEACHER or COACH?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) "cant come 2 practice, sry"
     ❌ Too casual and abbreviated for a teacher or coach. The
        tone doesn't fit the audience.
  B) "WHY DOES EVERYONE THINK I CAN COME TO EVERYTHING"
     ❌ ALL CAPS = shouting. Also, this tone is accusatory, not
        respectful.
  C) "Hi Coach, I won't be able to attend practice today due to  ✓ CORRECT
     a family commitment. I'll be there Wednesday. Thank you."
     ✅ Formal, respectful, specific — tone matches the
        audience. Includes greeting, reason, plan, and thanks.
        (SC.6.CC.1.1)
  D) "not coming today 🙃"
     ❌ Too curt and too casual. Missing greeting, reason, and
        any courtesy for a coach or teacher.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q5. The classical virtue of PRUDENCE, applied to digital
    communication, means:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Being brave enough to post your real opinions
     ❌ That's closer to fortitude (courage), not prudence.
  B) Wisely choosing the right action at the right moment —      ✓ CORRECT
     the right tool, audience, and timing
     ✅ Exactly! Prudence is practical wisdom. Online, it's
        asking "Is this the right channel? Right time? Right
        audience?" before acting. (SC.6.CC.1.1)
  C) Treating every person fairly and equally
     ❌ That describes the virtue of justice — also important,
        but a different virtue.
  D) Staying calm when you're angry
     ❌ Close! That's the virtue of TEMPERANCE (self-control),
        not prudence (wise choice). Prudence decides; temperance
        restrains.
