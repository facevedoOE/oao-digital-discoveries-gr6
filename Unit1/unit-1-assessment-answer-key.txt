Q1. (Wk 1, SC.6.CC.1.1)
    Which is the BEST example of ASYNCHRONOUS communication?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) A live Zoom class
     ❌ Zoom is synchronous — everyone is present at the same time.
  B) An email read the next morning                              ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Right! Email is the classic asynchronous example —
        sender and receiver are not online simultaneously.
  C) A live video call with grandma
     ❌ Video calls are synchronous.
  D) A voice chat during an online game
     ❌ Voice chat is synchronous — both parties speak in real time.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q2. (Wk 1, SC.6.CC.1.1)
    What is a DATA PACKET?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) A folder that stores all of your digital files
     ❌ That describes storage, not a packet.
  B) A type of router that connects devices
     ❌ Routers DIRECT packets — they aren't packets themselves.
  C) A small chunk of a message that travels across a network    ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Data packets are tiny pieces of a message that travel
        separately and are reassembled at the destination.
  D) A password that protects your messages
     ❌ That describes encryption, not a packet.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q3. (Wk 1, SC.6.CC.1.1)
    You need to send your science teacher a detailed question
    about an upcoming project. Which channel is MOST appropriate?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) A quick text with abbreviations
     ❌ Too informal for a teacher and not a school-appropriate
        channel for class questions.
  B) An email written in a clear, polite tone                    ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Email is asynchronous, professional, and leaves a written
        record — ideal for a detailed teacher question.
  C) A reaction emoji on a class post
     ❌ An emoji can't carry a full question.
  D) A live video call at midnight
     ❌ Synchronous calls require both people present — and late
        at night isn't appropriate for a teacher.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q4. (Wk 2, SC.6.CC.1.1)
    Which version of "I can't make practice today" has the BEST
    tone for a TEACHER OR COACH?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) "cant come 2 practice, sry"
     ❌ Too casual and abbreviated for a teacher or coach.
  B) "WHY DOES EVERYONE THINK I CAN COME TO EVERYTHING"
     ❌ ALL CAPS = shouting; tone is also accusatory.
  C) "Hi Coach, I won't be able to attend practice today due    ✓ CORRECT
     to a family commitment. I'll be there Wednesday. Thank you."
     ✅ Formal, respectful, specific — tone matches the audience.
        Includes greeting, reason, plan, and thanks.
  D) "not coming today 🙃"
     ❌ Too curt and too casual; missing greeting and courtesy.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q5. (Wk 2, SC.6.CC.1.1)
    Sofia wants to clap back at someone she disagrees with online.
    She closes the app and decides to think about it overnight
    instead. Which classical virtue is she practicing MOST?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Justice
     ❌ Justice is fairness — that's not what Sofia is showing.
  B) Temperance                                                  ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Temperance is self-control and restraint, especially
        when emotions run hot. Sofia paused before reacting —
        textbook temperance.
  C) Prudence
     ❌ Close — prudence is choosing wisely. The specific skill
        Sofia shows (holding back strong feelings) is temperance.
        Prudence decides; temperance restrains.
  D) Fortitude
     ❌ Fortitude is courage. Sofia isn't being brave here; she's
        holding back.

Q6. (Wk 3, SC.6.CC.2.1)
    A 30-second video shows a hungry shelter dog with sad music
    and ends with "Adopt today. Save a life." What is the MAIN
    rhetorical purpose?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) To inform
     ❌ Some facts may appear, but the goal is action, not just
        teaching.
  B) To entertain
     ❌ Sad music + shelter dog isn't designed to amuse.
  C) To persuade                                                 ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Emotional visuals + music + a clear call to action =
        persuasion. The video wants viewers to do something.
  D) To confuse
     ❌ Confusion isn't a real rhetorical purpose. The three are
        inform, persuade, entertain.

Q7. (Wk 3, SC.6.CC.2.1)
    You're designing a slideshow for KINDERGARTEN students. Which
    choice shows the BEST audience awareness?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Long paragraphs of text on every slide
     ❌ Kindergarteners can't read paragraphs.
  B) A 20-page report with footnotes
     ❌ That fits a college audience, not 5-year-olds.
  C) Big, friendly images and one short sentence per slide       ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Audience-first design: big visuals + simple language match
        a kindergarten audience perfectly.
  D) A black-and-white scientific diagram with no labels
     ❌ No engagement and no help reading.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q8. (Wk 3, SC.6.CC.2.1)
    The MAIN reason designers create a STORYBOARD before opening
    any digital software is:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Storyboards are required by school rules
     ❌ Smart designers choose storyboards because they save time.
  B) Sketches are cheap to change; finished video work is        ✓ CORRECT
     expensive to redo
     ✅ Right! Catch problems while changes still cost seconds,
        not hours.
  C) Storyboards look prettier than the final project
     ❌ Storyboards are usually rough on purpose.
  D) Storyboards always have to be in color
     ❌ Storyboards are typically pencil sketches with notes.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q9. (Wk 4, SC.6.CC.2.1)
    AESTHETIC UNITY in a multimedia project means:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Every page uses different colors and fonts to keep things
     interesting
     ❌ The opposite! Variety on every page kills unity.
  B) Including as many colors and fonts as possible
     ❌ More isn't better. Aesthetic unity comes from RESTRAINT.
  C) Every page feels like part of the same project — same       ✓ CORRECT
     colors, same fonts, same layout style
     ✅ Aesthetic unity is the visual signal that one designer
        made one project.
  D) Using the most expensive design software available
     ❌ Tools don't create unity — discipline does.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q10. (Wk 4, SC.6.CC.2.1)
     Which of these is the STRONGEST piece of peer feedback?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) "Good job!"
     ❌ Vague — your classmate doesn't know what to keep doing.
  B) "On page 2, the text is hard to read against the photo —    ✓ CORRECT
     could you add a darker background behind the words?"
     ✅ Specific (names the page and problem), kind (offers a
        solution), and actionable. Classic strong Glow/Grow.
  C) "It's bad."
     ❌ Vague AND unkind.
  D) "Change everything."
     ❌ Overwhelming and not specific. Strong feedback names ONE
        thing at a time.

Q11. (Wk 5, SC.6.CO.1.1)
     A FILE EXTENSION (like .docx or .mp3) tells the computer:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) The author's name
     ❌ Authors aren't part of the extension.
  B) Where the file is stored
     ❌ Storage location is in the file path, not the extension.
  C) What kind of file it is and which app should open it        ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Extensions match files to apps. Without the extension,
        the computer often can't tell what to do.
  D) The size of the file
     ❌ File size is shown separately. Extensions describe TYPE.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q12. (Wk 5, SC.6.CO.1.1)
     A friend sends you a file called dragon-model.stl. What is
     this file MOST likely?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) A song about dragons
     ❌ Songs use audio extensions like .mp3, .wav.
  B) A 3D model — possibly for a 3D printer or a game            ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Right! .stl is a classic 3D model format used for 3D
        printing and CAD tools.
  C) A short video clip of a dragon
     ❌ Videos use .mp4, .mov, .webm.
  D) A typed essay about dragons
     ❌ Essays use document formats like .docx or .pdf.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q13. (Wk 5, SC.6.CO.1.1)
     Which file name BEST follows the 4 rules of good naming
     (specific, no spaces, dated/versioned, keeps extension)?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Untitled.docx
     ❌ Not specific. "Untitled" tells you nothing.
  B) final FINAL FINAL v2 (3) latest.docx
     ❌ Spaces, redundant words, confused versioning.
  C) civil-war-essay-v3-2026-04-27.docx                          ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Specific, no spaces, versioned and dated, and keeps
        the .docx extension.
  D) My File
     ❌ Not specific, has a space, AND no extension. Worst of
        all worlds.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q14. (Wk 6, SC.6.CO.1.1)
     CLOUD STORAGE means:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Files stored in the sky on actual clouds
     ❌ The name is metaphorical! The cloud is very physical.
  B) Files stored only on your phone
     ❌ Phone-only storage is local, not cloud.
  C) Files stored on far-away servers in data centers,           ✓ CORRECT
     accessible from any device with internet
     ✅ The cloud is real — data centers full of computers.
        Cloud files follow you across devices.
  D) A type of internet browser
     ❌ Browsers load websites — different thing entirely.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q15. (Wk 6, SC.6.CO.1.2)
     You're proofreading a friend's essay. You want to suggest
     edits WITHOUT permanently changing their words. Which feature
     should you use?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Real-time co-editing
     ❌ Co-editing changes the text directly. You don't want to
        overwrite your friend's words.
  B) Comments
     ❌ Comments are great for asking questions, but they don't
        SHOW the edit you're suggesting.
  C) Suggesting mode (Track Changes)                             ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Suggesting mode shows your edits as PROPOSED changes —
        your friend can accept or reject each one. Perfect for
        proofreading someone else's work.
  D) Export to PDF
     ❌ PDF export creates a locked file. You can't easily
        suggest edits inside a finished PDF.
