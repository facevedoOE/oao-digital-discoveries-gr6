───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q1. (W29) A digital pond model uses sliders for rainfall, sun,
    and temperature. The sliders represent the model's:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Outputs
     ❌ Outputs are what the model PRODUCES (water level, fish).
        Sliders are inputs you control.
  B) Variables — values that can change                        ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Sliders let users change values — that's a textbook
        variable. (SC.6.PE.3.1)
  C) Constants
     ❌ Constants don't change. Sliders are designed to.
  D) Conditions (IF/THEN rules)
     ❌ Conditions use the variables; they aren't the values
        themselves.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q2. (W30) Three users testing your bus-tracking app report a
    crash. BEST next step?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Ship it — three testers isn't a lot
     ❌ Shipping a known-broken product isn't engineering.
  B) Use the feedback to revise and iterate                    ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Test → Improve is the design loop. Real feedback gets
        a real fix and another round of testing. (SC.6.PE.3.2)
  C) Scrap the project
     ❌ One fixable bug isn't a failed project.
  D) Ignore the feedback
     ❌ User feedback is the most valuable input the design
        cycle has.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q3. (W31) Which task is BEST suited for a machine working alone?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Granting parole
     ❌ High-stakes ethical judgment requires a human in the
        loop.
  B) Sorting 500,000 photos by which contain a cat             ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ High-volume, low-judgment pattern recognition is
        exactly where machines shine alone. (SC.6.ET.3.2)
  C) Comforting a grieving family member
     ❌ Empathy is a uniquely human strength.
  D) Diagnosing a complicated medical case
     ❌ AI assists, but a human doctor stays in the loop —
        this is collaboration, not solo machine work.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q4. (W32) Friend used AI for entire essay. Which response
    BEST reflects FORTITUDE?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Use AI for your own essay too
     ❌ Same shortcut. Not fortitude.
  B) Say nothing
     ❌ Silence isn't fortitude.
  C) Choose to write your own essay even though it's harder    ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Fortitude = persevering through hard work even when an
        easier path is available. (SC.6.ET.2.2)
  D) Report anonymously without talking first
     ❌ May be motivated by something — but it isn't an example
        of fortitude practiced on yourself.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q5. (Synthesis) How does learning-based AI relate to the
    digital models from Week 29?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) No connection
     ❌ The whole Section 3 of W33 reading was this connection.
  B) A learning-based AI is a digital model whose conditions
     were learned from data instead of written by hand         ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Both have variables and outputs. The difference is just
        where the conditions came from. (SC.6.PE.3.1, ET.2.1)
  C) Learning-based AI replaces all earlier models
     ❌ Rules-based models still outperform learning-based for
        many tasks (calculators, thermostats). They coexist.
  D) Digital models can't have variables; only AI does
     ❌ Variables are foundational to ALL digital models.
