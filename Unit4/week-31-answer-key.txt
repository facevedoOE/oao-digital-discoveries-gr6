───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q1. Which is a real, everyday use of AI you've probably already
    encountered?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) A streaming service suggesting your next song or video    ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Recommendation systems learn from your activity to
        predict what you'll like next. (SC.6.ET.3.2)
  B) A wall calendar showing the date
     ❌ Printed object — no AI.
  C) A pencil sharpener
     ❌ Mechanical tool — no AI.
  D) A glow-in-the-dark sticker
     ❌ Chemistry, not AI.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q2. Which is a uniquely HUMAN strength AI doesn't match well?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Performing the same calculation a million times
     ❌ That's a machine strength — humans get tired.
  B) Empathy and ethical judgment in unfamiliar situations    ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Empathy and ethical judgment are uniquely human.
        (SC.6.ET.3.2)
  C) Spotting tiny patterns in millions of data points
     ❌ Machine strength.
  D) Operating 24 hours a day without rest
     ❌ Machine strength — humans need sleep.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q3. Which is a MACHINE strength humans cannot match alone?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Choosing the perfect comforting words for a child
     ❌ Empathy in real time = human strength.
  B) Reading thousands of pages and finding one specific
     phrase in a fraction of a second                          ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Searching huge text volumes at speed is a classic
        machine strength. (SC.6.ET.3.2)
  C) Resolving a moral dilemma between two friends
     ❌ Human territory.
  D) Inventing a new birthday tradition for your family
     ❌ Creativity in emotional context = human territory.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q4. Which scenario most clearly REQUIRES a human in the loop?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Sorting your camera roll into folders
     ❌ Low-stakes — automation alone is fine.
  B) A medical AI deciding to deny treatment based on a scan  ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Denying medical treatment is high-stakes — a human
        doctor must stay in the loop. (SC.6.ET.3.2)
  C) Auto-correct suggesting word fixes
     ❌ Low-stakes suggestion you can ignore.
  D) A music app shuffling your playlist
     ❌ Low-stakes — automation is fine.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Q5. Friend wants to use AI to write their college essay. Most
    PRUDENT response?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A) Yes — faster and easier
     ❌ Prudence asks "what could go wrong?" — friend could
        face dishonesty consequences and miss the learning.
  B) Encourage them to write it themselves so the admissions
     reader sees THEIR thinking                                ✓ CORRECT
     ✅ Prudence (wise foresight) recognizes the essay's whole
        point is to share the friend's thinking. (SC.6.ET.3.2)
  C) Yes, but remove the AI's name from the file
     ❌ Hiding the source doesn't change the deeper issue.
  D) Say nothing; not your problem
     ❌ Silence isn't prudence — a good friend offers careful
        counsel.
